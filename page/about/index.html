About

I am a doctoral candidate in UT’s Educational Psychology department. I have been a TA for statistical courses from basic to advanced, research design, as well as data analysis, simulation, and programming in R. Under the guidance of and in collaboration with Dr. James E. Pustejovsky, I have worked on research involving single-case design, the direct observation of behavior, meta-analysis, and generalized linear models. My dissertation, chaired by Dr. Tasha Beretvas and co-chaired by Dr. Pustejovsky involves examining the impact of response-guided designs on treatment effect estimates from single-case designs. In my work as a consultant for the College of Education’s research design and statistical analysis consulting unit, I offer graduate students feedback and guidance on their research design and appropriate statistical analysis, tutor them on the use of statistical software, and help them to interpret their output. At least once a semester, I also provide a free, 10-hour (typically 3 consecutive Fridays) introductory workshop in using R.
